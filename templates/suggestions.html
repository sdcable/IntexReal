Micronutrients and Chronic Kidney Disease

The symptoms and severity of Chronic Kidney Disease can be managed through lifestyle changes. One of the biggest changes patients with CKD can make is to their diet. A CKD patient's diet must be tailored to limit the consumption of certain macro and micronutrients, including: protien, phosphorus, potassium, sodium, and water. Additional conditions (like Type II Diabetes) can provide additional dietary complications, be sure to consult your physician before making extreme dietary changes.
Additional Condition Recommended Diet CKD Diet Conflicts
Cardiovascular Disease Many Options
  • Be aware of high protein
  • High Phosphorus: poultry, soy
  • High Sodium: canned fruit/vegetables, some bread/grain items
  • High Potassium: some fruits/vegetables

  • Be aware of high protein

  • High Potassium: some fruits/vegetables
